The origins

The history of Capoeira Angola is punctuated by notable moments and it is difficult to give a summary that does justice to the survival of this art which has gone through many pitfalls and overcome a long period of prohibition...

Mestre Pastinha

Vincente Ferreira Pastinha was born on April 5, 1889 and died on November 13, 1981. As a child, he was small and frail, his neighbor, an old Angolan, Benedito, taught him the basics of Capoeira. This is how he begins to practice and how he will later want to share his knowledge...

Mestre João Pequeno

After attending capoeira rodas, wanting to become more combative, Mestre João Pequeno began practicing Capoeira with Mestre Gilvenson, he later became one of the main disciples of Mestre Pastinha...

Mestre Jogo de Dentro

Mestre Jogo de Dentro trained to become a master of Capoeira Angola at the Centre Esportiva de Capoeira Angola of the legendary Mestre João Pequeno...

Capoeira Angola

What happens during the class?

Capoeira Angola is a game, a dance-combat. The practice of this art requires physical training (quite intense), where we also learn to play instruments and sign roda music. It is a theatrical and euphoric martial art.

Training

Individual practice, led by mestre Colette, includes a warm-up, rehearsing the basic movements, and perfecting the traditional Capoeira Angola sequences. In the second part of the class, we put into practice what we have learned by working with a partner.

Music

Traditionally, Capoeira Angola practice is accompanied by the music and singing. During the class we learn to play different musical instruments, such as the berimbau, atabaque, pandeiro, and sing capoeira songs.

Singing

Song lies in the heart of Capoeira Angola tradition. By following the classic capoeira songs we make the practice more dynamic and fun. We learn to sing the songs in Portuguese and understand their meaning.

Roda

Roda is the manifestation of the art of Capoeira. Two capoeiristas play the game in the center made by the spectators and the bateria of musicians. The roda is a place of exchange, improvisation, and respect. It is the moment to put into practice what we have learned during the class.

Grupo de Capoeira Semente do Jogo de Angola in Montréal

Mestre Colette

A pioneer of Capoeira Angola in Canada, Colette has been practicing since 1990. She has established and is now directing the Grupo de Capoeira Semente do Jogo de Angola in Montreal. Colette has also taught for many years at UQAM, McGill University, and YMCA.

Contramestre Nico

Nicolas began practicing Capoeira Angola with Colette in January 2000. In 2019, he moved to Quebec and set up a cell of the Grupo de Capoeira Semente do Jogo de Angola. In 2021, he became the first contramestre formed by Colette and the GCSJA cell in Montreal. brault_nicolas@yahoo.com

Joël

Joël has joined the group in 2023 and since then practices capoeira every day. He achieved very high level of proficiency in a very short time. Now he is a leading student in the group and is helping others to improve their skills. His dream is to become a contramestre one day.
